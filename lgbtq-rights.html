LGBTQ+ TERMS TO KNOW

BACKGROUND TERMS

  • LGBTQIA: Stand for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, intersex, and asexual/aromantic
  • Gender: The social defenition of what it means to be a woman, a man, or another gender identity (what the person feels like they are)
  • Sex: The biological differences between male and female
  • Being Out: An LGBTQ person who publicly recognizes their sex, sexuality, or gender
  • Closeted: An LGBTQ person who will not or cannot disclose their sex, sexuality, or gender publicly
  • Ally: A person who is not LGBTQ but uses their privilege to support LGBTQ people and promote equality

SEXUAL ORIENTATIONS

  • Lesbian: A woman who is attracted to women
  • Gay: A person who is attracted to people of thier same gender; typically used to describe men
  • Heterosexual (Straight): A person who is attracted to thier opposite gender (not inheriently a part of the LGBTQ community)
  • Bisexual: A person attracted to more than one gender
  • Pansexual: A person attracted to people regardless of gender
  • Queer: Someone who embraces identities and sexual orientations outside of mainstream heterosexual and gender norms
  • Asexual: A person who experiences no sexual attraction
  • Aromantic: A person who experiences little to no romantic attraction

GENDER AND SEX

  • Intersex: Born with sex characteristics such as genitals or chromosomes that do not fit the typical definitions of male or female
  • Cisgender: A person who identifies as the gender they were assigned at birth (not inherietly part of the LGBTQ community)
  • Transgender: A person who identifies and a gender other than the sex they were assigned at birth
  • Non-binary: A person who identifies as a gender outside of the male/female binary
  • Genderless/Agender: a person who identifies with no gender at all

A FIVE-MINUTE INTRODUCTION TO LGBTQ+ HISTORY IN AMERICA

LGBTQ+ HISTORY TIMELINE

  • 1924 - The Society for Human Rights is founded by Henry Gerber in Chicago. It is the first documented gay rights organization.
  • April 1952 - The American Psychiatric Association's diagnostic manual lists homosexuality as a sociopathic personality disturbance.
  • April 27, 1953 - President Dwight D. Eisenhower signs an executive order that bans homosexuals from working for the federal government, saying they are a security risk.
  • July 1961 - Illinois becomes the first state to decriminalize homosexuality by repealing their sodomy laws
  • June 28, 1969 - Police raid the Stonewall Inn in New York City. Protests and demonstrations begin, and it later becomes known as the impetus for the gay civil rights movement in the United States.
  • December 15, 1973 - the American Psychiatric Association removes homosexuality from its list of mental disorders
  • January 9, 1978 - Harvey Milk is inaugurated as San Francisco city supervisor, and is the first openly gay man to be elected to a political office in California
  • 1978- Gilbert Baker designs the now-iconic rainbow flag
  • November 30, 1993 - President Bill Clinton signs "don't ask, don't tell", a military policy directive that prohibits openly gay and lesbian Americans from serving in the military.
  • September 21, 1996 - President Clinton signs the Defense of Marriage Act, banning federal recognition of same-sex marriage and defining marriage as "a legal union between one man and one woman as husband and wife."
  • April 1997 - Comedian Ellen DeGeneres comes out as a lesbian on the cover of Time magazine, stating, "Yep, I'm Gay." DeGeneres' character, Ellen Morgan, on her self-titled TV series "Ellen," becomes the first leading character to come out on a prime-time network television show.
  • September 20, 2011 - "Don't Ask, Don't Tell" is repealed, ending a ban on gay men and lesbians from serving openly in the military.
  • September 4, 2012 - The Democratic Party becomes the first major US political party in history to publicly support same-sex marriage on a national platform at the Democratic National Convention.
  • June 26 - the Supreme Court rules that states cannot ban same-sex marriage
  • March 23, 2018 - The Trump administration announces a new policy that bans most transgender people from serving in military. After several court battles, the Supreme Court allows the ban to go into effect in January 2019.
  • June 15, 2020 - The Supreme Court rules that federal law protects LGBTQ workers from discrimination.

MENTAL HEALTH ISSUES IN THE LGBTQ+ COMMUNITY

In one study, lesbian, gay and bisexual youth who reported higher levels of family rejection during adolescence were:

  • 8.4 times more likely to report having attempted suicide when compared to thier peers who experienced no rejection
  • 5.9 times more likely to report high levels of depression when compared to thier peers who experienced no rejection
  • 3.4 times more likely to use illegal drugs when compared to thier peers who experienced no rejection
  • 3.4 times more likely to report having engaged in unprotected sexual intercourse when compared to thier peers who experienced no rejection

HOMELESSNESS OF LGBTQ+ YOUTH

As a result of family rejection, discrimination, criminalization and a host of other factors, LGBTQ youth represent as much as 40% of the homeless youth population. Of that population, studies indicate that as many as 60% are likely to attempt suicide. Black LGBTQ youth, specifically young black men, are at the highest risk of homelessness amoung LGBTQ youth

Homelessness can cause:

  • Harm to mental health, physical health, and lifelong trauma
  • An increased risk of sexual abuse and exploitation
  • An increased risk of chemical and alcohol dependency
  • social stigma and discrimination
  • shorter life expectancy
  • economic struggles
  • lower levels of education
